Personal Statement

I am a wildlife biologist and my career has focused on the ecology and conservation of seals in Alaska with an emphasis on potential impacts of climactic changes on abundance, trend, and movement ecology. Species of focus include harbor seals and the ice-associated seals (ribbon, spotted, bearded, and ringed seals). Research areas of interest include modeling of marine mammal movement and behavior from deployed bio-loggers, survey design and abundance estimation from aerial surveys, haul-out and dive behavior, and application of new technologies to improve ecological research. I am an expert in R and literate programming approaches (e.g. Rmarkdown) and I am increasingly focused on open science, reproducibility in ecological analysis, and improving scientific collaboration.

Throughout my career, the following principles and ideas have guided me:

  • Learning is a constant
  • Find the quantitative within the qualitative
  • Awareness of emerging technologies
  • Ecological science products should be F.A.I.R.
  • Scientific coding is an essential skill
  • Meaningful collaboration requires empathy

R Packages

2008-present crawl (https://github.com/NMML/crawl)

  • co-lead developer

The Correlated RAndom Walk Library of R functions was designed for fitting continuous-time correlated random walk (CTCRW) models with time indexed covariates. The model is fit using the Kalman-Filter on a state space version of the continuous-time stochastic movement process.

2021-present pathroutr (https://github.com/jmlondon/pathroutr)

  • lead developer

The goal of {pathroutr} is to provide functions for re-routing paths that cross land around barrier polygons. The use case in mind is movement paths derived from location estimates of marine animals. Due to error associated with these locations it is not uncommon for these tracks to cross land. The {pathroutr} package aims to provide a pragmatic and fast solution for re-routing these paths around land and along an efficient path. It is important to note any re-routed paths are not statistical models and may have unintended consequences and biases associated with them that depends on the study animal, study area, and error nature of the original data. Re-routed paths may also exceed the speed or physiological capabilities of the study species.

2014-present wcUtils (https://github.com/jmlondon/wcUtils)

  • lead developer

A package for import/export of Wildlife Computers tag data. {wcUtils} provides functionality for working with data from select (SPLASH, SPOT) Wildlife Computers satellite telemetry tags. The package relies on data files produced by the Wildlife Computers DAP program or downloaded from the Wildlife Computers Data Portal.

2017-present tidextractr (https://github.com/jmlondon/tidextractr)

  • lead developer

This package provides a basic interface with the XTide library for estimation of tidal covariates. The package was developed to support analysis of harbor seal aerial survey and haul-out behavior in Alaska. However, use is not limited to this situation. The package does not include the XTide library and, thus, the library and command line tool must be installed. A vignette will provide some guidance for installation. Users may wish to investigate the rtide package as an alternative that includes native support for some of the station locations. Special thanks and attribution to David Flater for development and support of the XTide library (http://www.flaterco.com/xtide/xtide.html).
