Personal Statement

Wildlife biologist responsible for collaborative design, implementation and management of research and analysis related to seal ecology and conservation in Alaska. Species of focus include harbor seals and the ice-associated seals (ribbon, spotted, bearded, and ringed seal). Research areas of interest include modeling of marine mammal movement and behavior from deployed tags, survey design and abundance estimation from aerial surveys, haul-out and dive behavior and application of new technologies to improve ecological research. Expertise in R and literate programming approaches (e.g.Â Rmarkdown) and am increasingly focussed on open science, reproducibility in ecological analysis, and serve as an Openscapes (https://www.openscapes.org/) advocate and mentor within AFSC and NMFS.
